Meet the Types of Preferred Stock

Not all preferred shares are created equal. Different types offer unique features that can significantly impact an investment's risk and reward profile.

Cumulative

If the company skips a dividend, it must pay all missed dividends to cumulative preferred holders before paying any to common stockholders. This is a key safety feature.

Convertible

Gives the holder the option to convert their preferred shares into a fixed number of common shares, offering the potential for capital appreciation if the company succeeds.

Callable

Allows the issuing company to buy back the shares at a set price after a certain date. This is a risk for investors, as high-yield shares may be redeemed by the issuer.

Participating

Allows holders to receive extra dividends beyond the fixed rate if the company hits certain performance targets, offering more upside potential than standard preferreds.

The Investor's Scorecard

✔Pros

  • Higher Dividend Yields: Typically offers higher and more stable income than common stock and many bonds.
  • Priority Status: Gets paid dividends and liquidation proceeds before common stockholders.
  • Lower Volatility: Generally less price fluctuation than common stock.
  • Tax Advantages: Dividends often qualify for lower tax rates than bond interest.

✖Cons

  • Interest Rate Risk: Value typically falls when interest rates rise.
  • Limited Growth: Lacks the high capital appreciation potential of common stock.
  • No Voting Rights: No say in how the company is run.
  • Call Risk: Issuer can redeem the shares, forcing reinvestment at potentially lower rates.

Who Issues Preferred Stock?

The market is heavily concentrated in regulated industries that use preferred stock to meet capital requirements, providing financial stability.

Understanding the Key Risk: Interest Rates

The value of a fixed-dividend preferred stock has an inverse relationship with prevailing interest rates. When new, safer bonds offer higher yields, existing preferred shares become less attractive.
